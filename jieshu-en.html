Kessoku Band: Shimokitazawa Field Notes

[IKUYO NEWS Archive] Kessoku Band is a four-piece band active at Shimokitazawa STARRY. Their shows are known for youth, nerves, explosive energy, and occasional financial emergencies.

The band is built around guitar, vocals, bass, and drums. They may look one awkward conversation or empty wallet away from collapse, but on stage they turn that chaos into songs.

Hitori Gotoh

Role: Lead guitar / Instrument: Electric guitar

Hitori Gotoh is Kessoku Band's lead guitarist and the real person behind the online name Guitar Hero. Off stage she is shy, anxious, and likely to retreat into the nearest corner, but her guitar playing carries a surprising amount of force.

Her parts give the band its sharp edge and emotional release. When a solo opens up, it often sounds like every sentence she could not say out loud finally found a route to the audience.

Ikuyo Kita

Role: Vocalist and rhythm guitar / Instrument: Electric guitar

Ikuyo Kita is the vocalist and the brightest signal on stage. Her voice pulls the room toward the band, while her rhythm guitar keeps the song moving with a clear, energetic push.

Her guitar skills started behind the others, but she grows fast. Kita gives Kessoku Band a visible center: cheerful, direct, and dangerously radiant under STARRY's lights.

Ryo Yamada

Role: Bass / Instrument: Electric bass

Ryo Yamada is the bassist: quiet, independent, musically sharp, and almost permanently short on money. Her bass lines do more than follow the beat; they carve cool, distinctive paths under the song.

She rarely explains herself, but her tone and arrangements speak clearly. Many of Kessoku Band's coolest moments begin when Ryo lets the bass step forward.

Nijika Ijichi

Role: Drums / Instrument: Drum kit

Nijika Ijichi is the drummer and the person who truly holds the band together. She keeps the rhythm, the rehearsals, the communication, and often the general survival of the group on track.

Her drumming does not need to shout to be essential. For a band frequently pulled around by nerves and sudden incidents, Nijika is both the metronome and the steering wheel.
